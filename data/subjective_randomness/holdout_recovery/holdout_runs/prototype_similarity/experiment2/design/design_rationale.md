# Design Rationale — prototype_similarity Experiment 2

## Overview

**20 stimulus pairs selected** from a pool of 289 candidates by maximizing Expected
Information Gain (EIG) over five competing cognitive models.  EIG was computed from
prior-predictive `p_left` samples (no MCMC fitting), averaged over the model prior
defined in `model_registry.yaml`.

**EIG range:** 0.200 – 0.220 nats (all positive).

## Candidate pool construction

The 289 candidates were generated by five strategies, each targeting a distinct
discriminating axis among the five models:

1. **Symmetric-deviation pairs** (strategy 1, 70 pairs): one streaky sequence and
   one over-alternating sequence with the same |p\_alts − 0.5|.  These directly
   test `asymmetric_alternation_prototype` against `inner_loop_model`: the symmetric
   model predicts equal non-randomness for both; the asymmetric model predicts the
   streaky sequence looks less random (higher `streak_k` penalty).

2. **Mixed-length pairs** (strategy 2, 60 pairs): sequences with the same p\_alts and
   imbalance profile but different lengths (n=4 vs n=7/8, n=5 vs n=8).  These
   discriminate `length_sensitive_prototype` from `inner_loop_model`: the length-
   sensitive model penalises the longer sequence more heavily (sqrt(n) scaling),
   while the base model is agnostic to length.

3. **Compressibility pairs** (strategy 3): high periodicity/max\_run vs aperiodic
   sequences at similar imbalance — targeting `encoding_compressibility`.

4. **Bayesian-diagnosticity pairs** (strategy 4): biased (high imbalance) vs
   alternating (high p\_alts, low imbalance) sequences — targeting the generator-type
   scoring in `bayesian_diagnosticity`.

5. **Classic maximum-deviation pairs** (strategy 5): fully streaky vs fully
   alternating at lengths 4–8 — universally informative baseline.

## Selected stimuli

The top-20 stimuli are all extreme streaky-vs-alternating pairs spanning lengths 4–8:

| Length | Pair type                         | EIG      |
|--------|-----------------------------------|----------|
| 8      | all-H / all-T vs perfect alternation | 0.2196 |
| 6      | all-H / all-T vs perfect alternation | 0.2164 |
| 4      | all-H / all-T vs perfect alternation | 0.2110 |
| 7      | all-H / all-T vs perfect alternation | 0.2085 |
| 8      | all-H / all-T vs near-alternation    | 0.2034 |
| 5      | all-H / all-T vs perfect alternation | 0.2004 |

## Why these pairs are informative

Extreme streaky-vs-alternating pairs at multiple lengths simultaneously probe:

- **Asymmetric penalty** (`asymmetric_alternation_prototype`): fully streaky sequences
  sit at the maximum of the asymmetric region (p\_alts = 0); the model predicts they
  look significantly less random than a symmetrically deviant alternating sequence.
  The symmetric models (`inner_loop_model`, `length_sensitive_prototype`) predict the
  two sequences are near-equiprobable.

- **Length scaling** (`length_sensitive_prototype`): by covering lengths 4–8, the
  design allows the model to distinguish a mild length effect from none.  Pairs at
  the same extreme deviation but different lengths (e.g. HHHH vs HHHHHHHH) are
  present in the pool but scored lower than same-length extremes; the EIG ordering
  reflects that within-length extreme pairs are marginally more diagnostic.

- **Compressibility vs prototype** (`encoding_compressibility`): fully alternating
  sequences are maximally periodic, giving the compressibility model a high penalty
  while the prototype model penalises them only for alternation rate.

- **Bayesian diagnosticity** (`bayesian_diagnosticity`): extreme alternating sequences
  are maximally diagnostic of an alternating generator (log P(HTHTHTHT | alternating)
  >> log P(HTHTHTHT | fair)), while fully streaky sequences are similarly diagnostic
  of a streaky generator.

Together the 20 trials provide broad coverage of the model-discriminating space while
remaining achievable in ≈5 minutes at ≈5 seconds per trial.
