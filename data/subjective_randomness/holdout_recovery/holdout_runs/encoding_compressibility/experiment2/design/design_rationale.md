# Experiment 2 Design Rationale

**Number of stimuli:** 30 pairs
**EIG range:** 0.0824 – 0.1318

This design selects pairs that maximally discriminate between the five candidate cognitive models (`prototype_similarity`, `bayesian_diagnosticity`, `inner_loop_model`, `extended_compressibility`, and `alternation_and_run`).

The selected stimuli discriminate between the models in several key ways:
1. **Length discrepancies (e.g., "H" vs "HHTTTHT"):** Theories differ in how sequence length impacts perceived randomness. For instance, `bayesian_diagnosticity` may treat very short sequences as less diagnostic of a random generator, whereas models based purely on proportions (e.g., alternation rate) might evaluate them differently.
2. **Extreme homogeneity vs. perfect alternation (e.g., "THTHTHTH" vs "HHHHHHHH"):** Models such as `alternation_and_run` are highly sensitive to alternation rate and max run length, while compressibility-based models might find both highly compressible (thus non-random) but for different reasons.
3. **Homogeneous vs. periodic patterns (e.g., "HHHHHHHH" vs "HHTTHHTT"):** This contrasts theories that explicitly represent periodicity (`extended_compressibility`, `inner_loop_model`) against those that simply count runs or alternations.
4. **Moderate randomness vs. extreme regularity:** Pairs like "TTTTTTT" vs "THHTTTH" force models to decide whether sequences with some imbalance and runs are perceived as more random than perfectly balanced but non-random structures.
