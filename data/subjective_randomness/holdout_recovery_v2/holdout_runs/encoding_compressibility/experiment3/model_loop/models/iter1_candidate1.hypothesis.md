People judge a sequence as more random when its alternation rate is closer to a subjective ideal — an internal standard for how often a "typical" random sequence changes between heads and tails. When comparing two sequences, they prefer the one whose alternation rate deviates less from this ideal, treating both too much streakiness and too much regularity as evidence of non-randomness. The ideal rate is a free parameter, allowing the model to capture whether people's internal standard sits at, above, or below the true fair-coin rate of 0.5.
