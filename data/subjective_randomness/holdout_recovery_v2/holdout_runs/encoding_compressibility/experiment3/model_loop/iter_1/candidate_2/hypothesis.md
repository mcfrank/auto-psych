People judge a sequence as more random when its Shannon entropy is higher — that is, when its head proportion is closest to 0.5 in information-theoretic terms. Unlike a linear balance model, entropy penalizes extreme imbalance (p near 0 or 1) disproportionately more than mild imbalance (p near 0.4 or 0.6), so only strongly biased sequences lose randomness credit while mildly unequal sequences are treated as nearly equivalent to balanced ones. This is a single-cue, head-proportion-only model that refines the linear balance hypothesis with a specific nonlinear (entropy) scaling.
