People judge a sequence as more random when its longest consecutive run is close to the length expected from a fair coin of that length — approximately log₂(n). A max run that is too long signals a streaky, non-random process, but a max run that is too short signals an artificially regular, over-alternating process; both extremes feel non-random. Perceived randomness therefore peaks at the characteristic max-run length of a fair coin and declines quadratically as the actual max run departs in either direction.
