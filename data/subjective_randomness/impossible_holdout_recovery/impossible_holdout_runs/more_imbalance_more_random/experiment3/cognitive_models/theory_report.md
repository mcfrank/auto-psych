# Theory Report — Experiment 3

## imbalance_with_lapse
**Hypothesis:** People judge sequence randomness based purely on outcome frequencies, perceiving sequences with a greater proportion imbalance as more random, but their judgments are moderated by a constant lapse rate representing random guessing.
**Motivation:** The `iter1_candidate2` model found during the inner loop was statistically indistinguishable from the top model (`inner_loop_model`, elpd_diff=0.67), suggesting that a lapse rate might be a robust cognitive mechanism moderating choices alongside imbalance.
**Mechanism:** Implements exactly the imbalance-based heuristic of the top model but adds a `lapse` parameter, drawn from a Beta prior favoring low lapse rates, to mix the model's choices with a 50/50 guess.

## quadratic_imbalance
**Hypothesis:** People judge sequence randomness based on outcome frequencies, with perceived randomness growing quadratically as the proportion of heads and tails deviates further from a balanced distribution.
**Motivation:** The `iter0_candidate0` model generated by the inner loop tested this quadratic scaling. While it performed worse than linear imbalance (elpd_diff=36.29), formally adding it to the theory pool ensures we test different functional forms of the imbalance heuristic.
**Mechanism:** Compares sequences strictly by squaring the distance of their head/tail proportion from 0.5. It is distinct from `inner_loop_model` because it assumes the perceptual impact of imbalance accelerates for more extreme biases rather than scaling linearly.
