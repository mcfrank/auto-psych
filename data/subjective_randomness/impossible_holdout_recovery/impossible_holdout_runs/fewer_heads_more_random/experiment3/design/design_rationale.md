# Experiment 3 Design Rationale

- **Number of stimuli**: 30 pairs
- **EIG range**: 0.5062 – 0.6527
- **Design strategy**:
  To cleanly discriminate between the most competitive models from Experiment 2 (which apply linear, quadratic, and logarithmic penalties based on the absolute number of heads), we generated 300 random candidate sequence pairs varying in length (3 to 8) and sequence patterns. 
  The 30 stimuli selected by the EIG criterion strongly pit sequences with zero heads (e.g., `TTTTT`) against sequences with many heads but different lengths and alternation rates. This targets the structural differences between the leading theoretical models, determining exactly how the penalty for observing heads scales (linearly vs non-linearly) and validating whether length impacts the randomness judgment.
